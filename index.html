It’s Never Too Late To Change Your Life

Most people massively underestimate how much they’re capable of.
You and I experience the exact same doubts, fears, and anxieties.
The good news is, so does everyone.
Obviously some people are better at dealing with it while others are more naturally talented.
But I promise you, everyone is dealing with some level of imposter syndrome.
From what I’ve seen, the older you get, the more pronounced it becomes.
Even as a 25-year-old, I’m tempted to think I’m too old for some things.
This is clearly rubbish and is just my mind playing tricks on me.

Here’s the truth:
Success is not determined by age; it’s a mindset.
Whether you’re 25, 30, 40, or beyond, you will never be too old to do something new.
Obviously within reason.
I’m not saying you can turn 50 and decide to become an Olympic champion.
Please dear reader, use your common sense.
Clearly the earlier you start, the better your chances of changing are.
But I don’t want you to be despondent and depressed about not starting sooner.
If you’re in your 20s, you’ve got plenty of time.
If you’re in your 30s, you also have plenty of time.
Even if you’re in your 40s, you still have a lot of time.
So don’t feel bad for not changing yet.
Because you 100% can.
Unless you’re cognitively diminished and in your 80s, age is irrelevant.
It’s time to shift your perspective and realize that there are so many opportunities out there, just waiting for you to seize them.

Here are some tips to give you an unfair advantage:

Play to Your Strengths:
Everyone has unique talents and strengths that can be harnessed to pave the way to success.
You may not know what these are yet, but they exist.
Don’t try to be someone you’re not when you can leverage who you already are.
Identify what you excel at and use it as a launching pad for your pursuits.
In addition, if you don’t know what you’re good at, ask the people closest to you.
They’ll be able to cover your blind spots by pointing out what you might be unconscious of.
Your age should not limit you in this domain.
In fact, being older can give you an unfair advantage.
How?
Because you’ll have so many more experiences to draw from.
Most of the experiences you’ll have will be completely novel to you and won’t be found anywhere else.
Use these.
Embrace your own lessons and let them guide you towards your goals.

Understand the Game You’re Trying to Play:
Life is a game with its own rules.
And every area of life you decide to pursue has its own games with their own rules.
Before diving headfirst into a new venture, take the time to understand the intricacies of the game you want to play.
Research, observe, and immerse yourself in the field you wish to explore.
For example, music.
If you want to start making music, you’ll need to immerse yourself in its unique world.
You’ll need to learn how to play an instrument.
You’ll need to figure out what audience you want to create for.
You’ll need to become comfortable with the industry's technology as well as its culture.
Take your time with this step and don’t skip it.
Age may bring different perspectives, but it doesn’t hinder your ability to learn and adapt.
Follow your natural curiosities and become a devout student.

Focus on the Process, Not the Outcome:
Many people get caught up in the desire for immediate results.
Me included.
If anything, my generation is the worst at this.
We’ve been conditioned to expect everything to arrive immediately.
So much so that we suck at a lot of things.
Our attention spans are nonexistent and our pain tolerance is close to zero.
We’re too focused on quick and easy results as opposed to a pursuit of mastery.
Instead, you should shift your focus to the journey itself.
This will give you a significant edge over young bucks my age.
Success is not an overnight phenomenon; it’s a culmination of small victories and constant effort.
In the words of MJ Demarco, “Success is a process, not an event”.
Learn to love the process, savor each step forward, and move on from your setbacks.
When you focus on the present moment and the progress you’re making, it doesn’t matter how old you are.
Plus, your experience of time will change.
Instead of focusing on an imaginary future with arbitrary deadlines, you can just focus on the project at hand.

Give Yourself Time:
Don’t rush.
Rome wasn’t built in a day, and neither will your new path to success.
Give yourself permission to take it one step at a time.
It’s easy to fall into the trap of comparing yourself to others or feeling rushed because of age-related expectations.
Remember, change is a gradual process, and it’s never too late to start.
Patience and persistence will be your guiding lights on this transformative journey.
It doesn’t matter how old you are.
You can’t change that.
All you can change is your output.
Focus on your actions instead of your age.
You can’t get back any of the time that’s already passed.
Therefore, worrying about it is completely pointless.
Accept that all you have is now and get on with your work.

I hope this helps you.
